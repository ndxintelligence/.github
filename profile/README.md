# NDX Intelligence

NDX Intelligence is an independent research and documentation lab focused on Data Science and Artificial Intelligence.

The lab exists to support open research without gatekeeping by background, pedigree, or formal labels. Work is judged by the clarity of its reasoning, the explicitness of its assumptions, and the reproducibility of its results — not by where it originates or how it is presented.

## Research Philosophy

Research at NDX Intelligence is treated as a process rather than a set of outcomes. Every project begins with an explicit research question and proceeds through documented assumptions, experiments, failures, revisions, and interpretations. Results are not cleaned up retroactively, and unsuccessful paths are preserved alongside successful ones.

The goal is not speed, visibility, or polish. The goal is to leave behind research trails that can be followed, questioned, and reproduced over time.

## Structure of the Work

The work at NDX Intelligence is organized deliberately:

- **ndx-systems**  
  Shared research infrastructure. This repository contains system-level tooling, evaluation logic, and methodological standards used across all projects. It evolves slowly and is never discarded.

- **ndx-project-\***  
  Each project repository represents a single research question and a single paper trajectory. Projects are self-contained, independently readable, and linked conceptually and technically to `ndx-systems`.

- **ndx-meta**  
  Cross-project synthesis, longitudinal reflection, and documentation of how methods and beliefs evolve over time.

- **ndx-archive**  
  Completed, abandoned, or deprecated work preserved for long-term reference.

GitHub is treated as the authoritative research record. Code, experiments, analyses, failures, and paper drafts live here. Other platforms exist only to index and explain this work, not to replace it.

## How to Read This Work

Readers are encouraged to start with a project’s README and research question, then follow the documented trail through experiments, results, analysis, and paper drafts. Failures and rejected alternatives are preserved intentionally and should be read as part of the research record, not as noise.

This organization is not a portfolio, a product repository, or a services catalog. It is a public research record designed to remain interpretable over time.
